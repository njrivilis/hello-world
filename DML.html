Digital Media and Literacy

Digital Media and Literacy is designed to be a short middle school course to introduce students to topics in computer science and related technology. Students learn to make video games and animations with Scratch, design 3D models with TinkerCAD, and program 3D-printed robots to complete various challenges. Addtionally, the course is an introduction to how computers work and how to stay safe online, as well as modern topics such as fake news and artificial intelligence.

This course was first created at Arlington Public Schools, with Terry Dash, Matt Larrabee, and Matt Coleman. I taught it and created these resources over school years 2017-2018 and 2018-2019. The course is currently being taught by Adia Wallace, who can be contacted at awallace@arlington.k12.ma.us
