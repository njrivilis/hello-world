Fundamentals of Computer Science

This course is designed to introduce students to foundational topics in computer science, such as parts of a computer, how the internet works, and beginner skills in JavaScript programming. The curriculum is designed to strongly overlap with the AP Computer Science Principles material. There are many quizzes built into this work, so that students can prepare for AP level exam questions and learn broad test taking strategies for MCAS, SAT, and future college and career-related exams.

This course has 8 main units:
  1. Digital Data Representation
  2. Computer Organization
  3. The Internet
  4. Programming with Variables
  5. Procedures
  6. Data Structures and Algorithms
  7. Computer Collaboration and Global Impact


Any of these topics could be expanded to a full semester course, but this currciulum is designed to expose students to the general principles at an introductory level.
